Recycle at Christmas

It's that time of year again. There are spiced lattes abound in the coffee shops, and twinkling lights adorn George Square. Christmas is a wonderful time that always seems to be over so quickly! Soon enough, it is time to dismantle the Christmas tree masterpiece from our living rooms, and figure out what to do with its browning, bare boughs.



Recycle your Christmas Tree

You can add a touch of eco-consciousness to this festive period by disposing of your Christmas tree the green way.

Glasgow City Council advises real Christmas trees can be placed in your brown bins for kerbside collection. To ensure collection, please cut the tree into pieces and check that the lid will close.

Alternatively, trees can be taken to any of the following household council recycling centres:[2]

Recycle your Wrapping Paper

In the UK, 5 million tonnes of paper is sent to landfills every year.[3] With increasing trends in foil and glittered papers, it is unlikely that the material is %100 paper. Many papers contain small amounts of plastic and metals that cannot be recycled.

To ensure that your wrapping paper is recyclable, you can do the "Scrunch Test". This involves scrunching your paper into a ball. If it remains scrunched, then it's likely you can recycle your paper. If it un-scrunches, it cannot.

Even if your paper is recyclable, you will still need to remove all sellotape and gift tags that cannot be recycled!

Plastic Free Fridays

We can do our part to tackle the plastic crisis that is affecting our planet by using less plastic. An excellent start to this is by taking part in #PlasticFreeFriday.

Plastic pollution sticks around for ages with horrible effects on wildlife and the environment. Every minute a truckload of our plastic enters our oceans. Sea creatures can get tangled up in it or mistake it for food.

But we can all do our bit to help! Going plastic-free on Fridays is a small step you can take to make a real difference to the amount of single-use plastic that ends up in the ocean. Whether it’s buying shampoo bars or drinking from a reusable water bottle, there are loads of easy ways to start cutting out plastic.

Recycle in the Garden



Many household items can be repurposed and reused to suit the needs of the green-thumbed.


  • Plastic bottles can be cut in half to make mini-cloche which will protect seedlins from winds, slugs and snails. They can also be used as watering cans.[4]
  • Plastic milk cartons can be used as a soil scoop when the bottom is cut off.[5]
  • Plastic punnets make perfect mini propagator lids when turned upside down.[5]
  • Disks threaded on string and attached to branches or the washing line make excellent bird scarers.[4]
  • Lollipop sticks can be used as garden markers for labelling plants.[5]
  • Cardboard egg cartons are perfectly designed for chitting seed potatoes.[5]
  • Toilet roll tubes are perfect for starting peas, sweet peas, carrots and other crops that require a deep rooting area. Simply fill them with compost and sow seeds into the top of the tube. When you transplant them outdoors, simply plant the entire tube in the ground and let it decompose in the soil.[5]

By doing a few of these simple things, you can minimise tge amount of plastics you send to landfills!




Recycle in the Closet

Textiles and shoes account for more than 12% of all waste in landfill sites. If everyone in the UK bought just one reclaimed woollen jumper each year we would save roughly 400 million gallons of water. This is more than the volume of the average UK reservoir.

Recycling and reusing clothing can benefit the environment in so many ways. Check out our Ethical Shopping page to find out more about shopping for the environment. Furthermore, below is a list of textile recycling companies who send your unwanted clothing to those in need.

Clyde Recycling Ltd

Clyde Recycling Ltd is a professional clothing and textiles recycling company with over 30 years industry leading experience. From their facility in Glasgow, they recycle high quality second hand clothing and export to all over the world. Last year Clyde Recycling shipped over 3000 tonnes of clothing across Africa, Europe and South America.

Cash4Clothes

Cash4Clothes is a company that purchases your unwanted clothes and sends them on to be reused! You can organise a collection, or bring your clothes in to a local branch, and you can walk away with cash! All of your clothes get exported worldwide where it will get a new lease of life and worn again.

Recycle at School

By following the ideas beloe, you can easily reduce the amount of waste going to landfill, all during a school day!

  • Reduce your paper use by keeping a scrap paper tray in each classroom or even switching to recycled paper.[7]
  • Reduce your plastic waste by bringing a refillable water bottle to school rather than single-use plastic bottles.[7]
  • All schools have battery collection points. Encourage your students to bring in their used batteries from home.[7]
  • Set up a student-led Eco-Team to help raise awareness and monitor waste and recycling thoghout the school.[7]
  • Be bold and make a statement! Set goals for your school to reduce paper/hand-towel use by 10%.[7]

Local Beach Cleans

An estimated 26 million tons of plastic pollution ends up in the oceans each year where it chokes, entangles and poisons marine life.

To tackle this global issue, organisations (such as the ones below) have been leading the charge against marine pollution by hosting voluntary Beach Cleans around the UK.


If you fancy swapping the sandals for wellies, and the bucket for a bin-bag, check out some of the links below and get involved!

  • Keep Scotland Beautiful

5-a-Day Litter Challenge

The British Sub-Aqua Club (BSAC) have been hosting events for their 'Marine Clean of 2018'. Their #5-a-DayLitterchallenge is for both beneath the waves and above them. Scuba-Divers and shore support crews around the UK have been taking part to help keep our oceans clean.

Whether it’s while you’re at work, out and about with friends or family, or simply walking the dog, try BSAC's Five-a-Day litter challenge, and you can make a difference to the health of our environment.

Do it – share it! From a photo of the litter you collect to a pic of your Underwater Litterpick or Beach Clean team in action, share your BSAC Marine Clean efforts on Instagram or Twitter using the hashtag #bsacmarineclean to show your support and help clean up your oceans.

WEEE Scotland

WEEE Scotland LTD provides a professional and cost effective electrical repair and recycling solutions for businesses and consumers throughout Scotland, the UK and Europe.

WEEE Scotland recycles any electrical equipment from Washing Machines, Fridges, TVs and Computers all the way to Scientific Instruments. At their Glasgow hub, they analyse all the electronic materials they receive, repairing equipment where they can, and dismantling products for component resale and recycling where they cannot.

Glasgow Wood Recycling

Glasgow Wood Recycling is a social enterprise and charity established in 2006. They are committed to reducing the amount of wood needlessly going to landfill by finding creative and socially inclusive ways to reuse this valuable natural resource, to make quality furniture and bespoke pieces for businesses and the home.

Through these activities, they provide volunteer and training opportunities to local people, as a practical way of tackling unemployment and social exclusion.



ReCONNECT Glasgow

ReCONNECT Glasgow was set-up by Connect Community Trust (link to CCT site), with the aim of helping reduce waste within Glasgow and to create local training and employment in the East End of Glasgow.

ReCONNECT Glasgow specialises in the reuse, recycling and upcycling of household items with the aim to provide durable, stylish, creative and reasonably priced items for reuse by individuals and companies. All while saving the environment!

4Ocean

4ocean is a global movement actively removing trash from the ocean and coastlines while inspiring individuals to work together for cleaner beaches and oceans, one pound at a time.

Made from recycled materials, every bracelet purchased funds the removal of 1 pound of trash from the ocean and coastlines. In less than 2 years, 4ocean has removed 2,331,951 pounds of debris from the ocean and coastlines.

Each month, 4ocean partner with various marine, ecological and environmental organisations to offer limited edition bracelets. A portion of the cost of these bracelets are given to the charities to help further their causes.

4ocean pledge to their customers that 4ocean will remove one pound of trash for one bracelet purchased from the ocean and coastlines within 12 months from the time of purchase.

The Ocean Cleanup

The Great Ocean Cleanup is a non-profit organisation that develops technilogies to assist in the clean up of the world's ocean plastic. A full-scale deployment of their clean up system is estimated to clean up 50% of the Great Pacific Garbage Patch in 5 years.

You can help support this awesome effort by making a donation, or by purchasing sustainable and ethically sourced merchandise from their online shop. Your contributions help aid the research that improves the technology, and assists in accelerating the largest cleanup in history.

If you are interested in funding your own cleanup system or making a large contribution, you can contact them through funding@theoceancleanup.com.
